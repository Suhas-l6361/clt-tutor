IPMAT — Integrated Programme in Management Aptitude Test

Choose a topic

The Integrated Programme in Management Aptitude Test (IPMAT) is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) for admission to their 5-year Integrated Programme in Management (IPM). The IPM is a unique dual-degree program combining undergraduate (BBA equivalent) and postgraduate (MBA) studies, designed for students who have completed Class 12 and aspire to pursue management early in their careers.

Introduced first by IIM Indore in 2011, the IPM model has since been adopted by other IIMs, including IIM Rohtak, IIM Ranchi, IIM Bodh Gaya, IIM Jammu, and IIM Kozhikode. The program blends foundation-level courses in economics, mathematics, social sciences, and humanities with advanced management education in the later years.

IPMAT serves as the gateway to India's most prestigious management institutes, offering an early entry route into IIMs without needing to appear for CAT later. It tests a candidate's aptitude in quantitative ability, verbal skills, and logical reasoning by assessing both analytical and communication strengths essential for future business leaders.

The 5-year IPM program grants students a Bachelor of Arts (Foundations of Management) or BBA, followed by an MBA, upon completion. Students also have the flexibility to exit after three years with a bachelor's degree.

Applicants must have completed Higher Secondary Education (10+2 or equivalent) from a recognized board with the required minimum marks.

The exam is conducted online (computer-based) at designated test centers across India.

Selection Process

Shortlisted candidates are invited for a Personal Interview (PI) based on their IPMAT scores. The final selection is made considering the Aptitude Test Score, PI performance, and past academic records.

Minimum marks

  • General/OBC/EWS candidates: Minimum 60% aggregate (or equivalent grade).
  • SC/ST/PwD candidates: Minimum 55% aggregate (or equivalent grade).

Final-year students of higher secondary education may also apply, but admission will remain provisional until they meet the required marks.

Selected candidates must submit final mark sheets/proof of qualification by December 31, 2025, failing which their admission will stand cancelled.

Additional Requirements:

  • A valid IPMAT score conducted by respective IIMs (such as IIM Indore, IIM Rohtak, etc.).
  • Proficiency in English, demonstrated through board examinations or standardized tests (for international applicants).

(Note: Relaxation in eligibility criteria applies as per Government of India and respective IIM norms.)

The IPMAT (Integrated Programme in Management Aptitude Test) is conducted by IIM Indore and IIM Rohtak separately, each following its own format. The test evaluates candidates on Quantitative Ability and Verbal Ability skills.

IIM Indore IPMAT Pattern

  • Total Sections: 3
    1. Quantitative Ability (Multiple Choice Questions)
    2. Quantitative Ability (Short Answer Questions)
    3. Verbal Ability (Multiple Choice Questions)
  • Duration: 120 minutes (40 minutes per section)
  • Marking Scheme: +4 for each correct answer, −1 for each incorrect MCQ (no negative marking for short answers)

IIM Rohtak IPMAT Pattern

  • Total Sections: 3
    1. Quantitative Ability
    2. Logical Reasoning
    3. Verbal Ability
  • Duration: 120 minutes
  • Marking Scheme: +4 for each correct answer, −1 for each incorrect answer

Mode of Examination

The exam is conducted online (computer-based) at designated test centers across India.

Selection Process

Shortlisted candidates are invited for a Personal Interview (PI) based on their IPMAT scores. The final selection is made considering the Aptitude Test Score, PI performance, and past academic records.

Application forms for IPMAT Indore and IPMAT Rohtak will be released on the respective official websites in February–March.

  • IPMAT Indore Application Portal: www.iimidr.ac.in
  • IPMAT Rohtak Application Portal: www.iimrohtak.ac.in

Application Fee:

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  1. What is IPMAT?

    The Integrated Program in Management Aptitude Test (IPMAT) is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) for admission to their 5-year Integrated Program in Management (IPM), which combines a BBA and MBA.

  2. Which IIMs accept IPMAT scores?

    Currently, IIM Indore and IIM Rohtak conduct their own versions of IPMAT. Other IIMs such as IIM Ranchi, IIM Jammu, and IIM Bodh Gaya also accept IPMAT (Indore/Rohtak) scores.

  3. Who can apply for IPMAT?

    Students who have completed or are appearing for Class 12 (10+2) or equivalent from a recognized board can apply. Minimum marks criteria vary by category — generally 60% for General/OBC/EWS and 55% for SC/ST/PwD.

  4. How is IPMAT different for IIM Indore and IIM Rohtak?

    Both have distinct exam patterns and selection processes:

    IIM Indore's IPMAT includes Quantitative Aptitude (MCQ + Short Answer) and Verbal Ability, followed by Personal Interviews.

    IIM Rohtak's version includes Quantitative Ability, Logical Reasoning, and Verbal Ability, with a subsequent Personal Interview round.

  5. Can students from non-commerce backgrounds apply?

    Yes, students from any academic stream (Science, Commerce, Arts, Humanities) can apply as long as they meet the eligibility criteria.

  6. What is the exam mode and duration?

    The IPMAT is conducted online (computer-based) with a duration of 2 hours at both IIM Indore and IIM Rohtak (see Paper Pattern for section timings).

  7. Is coaching necessary for IPMAT?

    While not mandatory, structured guidance and mock test practice help significantly, especially in the Quantitative Aptitude and Verbal Ability sections.

  8. When is IPMAT conducted?

    The exam is typically held in May or June each year, with applications opening around February–March.

  9. What happens after clearing the exam?

    Shortlisted candidates must appear for a Personal Interview (PI) and may be evaluated on academic performance, communication skills, and overall aptitude.

  10. How competitive is IPMAT?

    IPMAT is highly competitive, with thousands of applicants for limited seats. Early preparation, time management, and conceptual clarity are key to success.
